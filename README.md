# HR-PowerBi-Project
(Interactive Dashboard creation using MS Excel)

## Project Objective 
The objective of this project is to create an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard using Power BI that provides a full overview of workforce insights. It helps HR teams and management monitor key employee metrics such as total employees, gender ratio, service years, promotion status, job levels, and retrenchment data.
By visualizing these metrics, the dashboard enables data-driven decision-making, improves HR planning, and enhances organizational transparency.

## Questions (KPIs)
   
- Home Page – Key Questions
- What is the total number of employees, and how is the gender distribution divided between male and female?
- How many employees are currently on service versus those retrenched?
- What percentage of employees are due for promotion?
- Which service year category has the highest number of employees?
- How are employees distributed across different job levels and distance statuses?

- Action Page – Key Questions

- Which departments have the highest numbers of employees to be retrenched or promoted?
- How is job satisfaction distributed among employees (High, Medium, Low)?
- What percentage of employees work overtime, and how does it relate to their ratings?
- Which job roles have the highest counts of total employees, retrenchments, and promotions?
- What insights can HR derive from comparing retrenchment and promotion data across departments?

- Details Page – Key Questions

- Who are the employees due for promotion?
- Who are the employees that will be retrenched?
- What is the total count of employees in both promotion and retrenchment lists?

## Process
- Verify data for any missing values and anomalies, and sort out the same.
- Made sure data is consistent and clean with respect to data type, data format and values used.
- Created pivot tables according to the questions asked.
- Merge all pivot tables into one dashboard and apply slicer to make dynamic.

## Project Insight

- The organization employs 1,470 people, with a 60% male and 40% female distribution, showing moderate gender diversity.
- 92% of employees are currently active, while 8% have been retrenched, indicating stable workforce retention.
- Only 4.9% of employees are due for promotion, suggesting limited career advancement opportunities or recent promotions.
- Most employees have between 1 to 5 years of service, highlighting a relatively young or recently expanded workforce.
- The majority of employees live very close to the workplace, which could positively impact attendance and retention rates.
- The Research & Development department shows the highest retrenchment and promotion activity, indicating potential restructuring or skill alignment needs.
- Employees with high job satisfaction form the largest group, which reflects a generally positive work culture.
- Around 85% of employees are high-rated, showing strong overall performance levels.
- A small portion of employees work overtime, which may correlate with high performance or specific job roles.

## Final Conclusion:

The HR Analytics Dashboard provides valuable insights into workforce composition, performance, and satisfaction, enabling HR teams to make informed decisions. It highlights key areas such as promotions, retrenchments, and employee engagement, helping the organization maintain balance, improve retention, and enhance overall productivity through data-driven workforce planning.
